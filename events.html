Robocore

  • Resource: Mr.Salamon (Executive of Robotics Club of team 2021-22)
  • Date: 11th & 12th January, 2023
  • Description: Robotics club @ SIST in association with Centre for Robotics and Automation from School of Mechanical with 20 participants.
  • Agenda of the event:
  • Day 1:
  • • Intro to the Machines and Robot
    • Anatomy of Robot
    • Intro to ABB Robot-IRB1520ID
    • Installation of ROBOT STUDIO Software
    • Worked on a number of components in Robot Studio

  • Day 2:
  • • Hands-on session with Flex Pendant on robot axis movement
    • Automation with Flex Pendant Programming
  • The students participated in a two-day programme where they learnt about the axis movements, picking, and putting of objects at desired location using ABB robotic arm. We administered a little quiz at the conclusion of the session to gauge the degree of knowledge and keep things exciting. The participants were required to provide an evaluation of what they had learned. Circuitronics

  • Resource:Robotics club core Team
  • Date: 5th August, 2022
  • Description: Understanding fundamental circuits allows us to study more complex devices as engineers. The goal of this competition is to evaluate each participant's aptitude for circuit planning and problem-solving.
    In circuitronics, we had 3 Rounds:
    Technical Quiz
    The test was built on frequently asked topics from the Technology, Innovation, and Research categories. Each question had a set allotment of time. It was an objective question.
    Circuit Debugging
    Each team was given a circuit design, and they had a set amount of time to find any errors or weaknesses in the circuit. There were 5 points awarded for each right response. For incorrect responses, there are deducted points.
    Design Challenge
    On online platforms and EDA programmes like Tinkercad, KiCad, Proteus, and Fritzing, participants created their prototypes. Each design received a grade based on a number of factors, including prototype functionality, accuracy, design, and idea.

Arduicad

  • Resource:Robotics club core Team
  • Date: 25th July, 2022
  • Description:As the core team of 2022-23, we organized our first event, which turned out to be an insightful, hands-on experience for everyone involved. The event was centred around microcontrollers and the interfacing of sensors with them. To teach the participants, we used Tinkercad, an online platform that helped them connect various sensors and actuators to the microcontroller board. In addition, we provided an introduction to programming Arduino and the IDE software. The participants were delighted with the session, and it was an excellent opportunity for them to gain practical knowledge and hands-on experience in electronics. We look forward to hosting more such events in the future.

Wonderful opportunistic Workshop (Hands-on)

  • Topic: Getting Started with Development Boards
  • Resource: Robotics Club Core Team
  • Date: 3-5 & 8th March, 2022
  • Description: Robotics club @ SIST in association with Skill Development Centre conducted a workshop 'WOW' from 3rd to 5th & 8th March, 2022 with 35 participants.
  • Agenda of the event:
  • Day 1:
  • • Intro To The Embedded World.
    • Intro To World Famous Controller “Arduino Uno”
    • LED Blink With Arduino
    • Led Glow Using Push Button
    • Intro To Various Types Of Sensors Available
    • Covid-19 Door Bell Using Ir Sensor and Buzzer
    • Obstacle Avoider Using Ultrasonic and Motor
    • Smoke and Gas Detector Using Gas Sensor With Various Level of Indicator
  • Day 2:
  • • Smart Street Lighting System Using LDR
    • Keypad Based Motor Control
    • Bluetooth and Its Applications
    • Building Own App Using MIT App Inventor and Controlling Bluetooth
  • Day 3:
  • • Intro About IoT and Its Application
    • Esp8266 Interfacing With DHT Sensor and Uploading to ThingSpeak/Blynk
    • Voice Controlled With Google Assistant Using Nodemcu
  • We made the participants highly interactive as we conducted the entire workshop like a marathon, where each individual team was graded after the completion of each project. Also, points were given for both technical and non-technical games based on their performance. We distributed prizes to teams that performed well in games. After completion of all the technical parts, we made each team do their own innovative projects, and we evaluated them later. For the top 3 performing teams, we distributed metals, and for all participants, we distributed certificates. Core team, along with volunteers, made the event successful, and we got a good response from our participants.
